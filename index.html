What was the Holocaust?

The Holocaust, also known as the Shoah, was the systematic persecution and murder of approximately six million Jews by Nazi Germany and its collaborators during World War II. The Holocaust also targeted other groups, including Roma, people with disabilities, and homosexuals, among others.

The Holocaust was characterized by a series of state-sponsored policies and actions that sought to dehumanize and eliminate Jews and other "undesirable" groups from society. These policies included discriminatory laws, ghettos, forced labor camps, concentration camps, and ultimately extermination camps such as Auschwitz and Treblinka.
