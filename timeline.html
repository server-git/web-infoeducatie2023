Timeline of the Holocaust

  • 1933

    Adolf Hitler Becomes Chancellor of Germany

    Adolf Hitler rises to power as the Chancellor of Germany, laying the foundation for the implementation of Nazi policies.

  • 1934

    Hitler proclaims himself Leader and Reinch Chancellor

    Hitler proclaims himself FÃ¼hrer und Reichskanzler (Leader and Reich Chancellor). Armed forces must now swear allegiance to him

  • 1935

    Nuremberg Laws

    The Nuremberg Laws are enacted, stripping German Jews of their citizenship and implementing discriminatory measures against them.

  • 1936

    Rome-Berlin Axis

    Hitler and Mussolini form Rome-Berlin Axis

  • 1937

    Buchenwald

    Buchenwald concentration camp opens

  • 1938

    Kristallnacht

    Nov. 9-10: Kristallnacht (Night of Broken Glass): anti-Jewish pogrom in Germany, Austria, and the Sudetenland; 200 synagogues destroyed; 7,500 Jewish shops looted; 30,000 male Jews sent to concentration camps (Dachau, Buchenwald, Sachsenhausen)
    Nov. 12: Decree forcing all Jews to transfer retail businesses to Aryan hands
    Nov. 15: All Jewish pupils expelled from German schools

  • 1939

    Germany gets bigger

    Germans occupy Czechoslovakia

  • 1940

    France Surrenders

    Germans occupy Denmark and southern Norway
    France surrenders
    Rome-Berlin-Tokyo Axis

  • 1941

    United States declares war on Japan and Germany

    Anti-Jewish riots in Romania, led by the Iron Guard (Romanian fascist organization); hundreds of Jews butchered
    Germany invades the Soviet Union
    Japanese attack Pearl Harbor
    United States declares war on Japan and Germany

  • 1943

    German 6th Army surrenders

    German 6th Army surrenders at Stalingrad (Volgograd)

  • 1944

    Germany over Hungary

    Germany occupies Hungary
    Russians liberate Majdanek killing center
    Last Jews deported from Theresienstadt (Terezin) to Auschwitz

  • 1945

    End of Holocaust

    Evacuation of Auschwitz; beginning of death march
    Hitler commits suicide
    Bombing of Hiroshima
    Bombing of Nagasaki
    Japan surrenders; end of World War II
